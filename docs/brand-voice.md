## Voice vs. Tone

**Voice** is our personality‚Äîconsistent across every touchpoint.  
**Tone** adapts to context while staying true to our voice.

> Think of it this way: You have the same voice whether talking to your boss or your best friend‚Äîbut your tone changes.

---

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Knowledgeable‚Äîbut not gatekeepy

We know the collectibles world intimately. We speak the language. But we never make others feel small for not knowing.

**This:** "Patina refers to the surface aging that authenticates vintage pieces."  
**Not that:** "Only true collectors understand the value of proper patina."

**Do:** Share expertise generously. Explain terms when needed. Assume intelligence.  
**Don't:** Use jargon to exclude. Talk down. Assume everyone knows niche terminology.

---

### 2. Confident‚Äîbut not cocky

We know we're building something better than traditional auction houses. But we let the product speak for itself.

**This:** "3% fee. No hidden costs. No surprises."  
**Not that:** "We're the best platform ever created for collectibles."

**Do:** State facts plainly. Let data and results build credibility.  
**Don't:** Overpromise. Use hyperbole. Make claims we can't back up.

---

### 3. Exclusive‚Äîbut not snobby

We're curated by design. Not everyone gets in. But that selectivity serves our community, not our ego.

**This:** "Every collection is reviewed before listing. This keeps quality high."  
**Not that:** "Only elite sellers are welcome here."

**Do:** Celebrate curation as quality control. Welcome serious collectors warmly.  
**Don't:** Act superior. Create artificial scarcity. Make people feel unworthy.

---

### 4. Modern‚Äîbut not trendy

We bring Silicon Valley UX to a dusty industry. But we're not chasing every design fad.

**This:** "The interface you expect from modern apps, applied to serious collecting."  
**Not that:** "We're disrupting the antiquated auction industry with blockchain AI synergy."

**Do:** Highlight improvements over legacy systems. Show, don't tell.  
**Don't:** Use buzzwords. Promise "revolutionary" changes. Sound like a startup pitch deck.

---

### 5. Transparent‚Äîbut not blunt

We hide nothing: fees, processes, policies. But we communicate with care, not coldness.

**This:** "If your item doesn't sell, you pay nothing. Here's why that might happen..."  
**Not that:** "Most items sell. Some don't. That's business."

**Do:** Explain the "why" behind policies. Admit limitations openly.  
**Don't:** Hide bad news in fine print. Be robotic. Avoid difficult conversations.

## Tone Adaptation Matrix

### By Channel

| Channel | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| **Homepage/Marketing** | Confident, benefit-focused | "Keep 97% of every sale. Sell in 7 days." |
| **Product UI** | Concise, action-oriented | "Place bid" not "Submit your bidding offer" |
| **Email (sellers)** | Direct, results-focused | "Your collection has 12 active bidders" |
| **Email (buyers)** | Enthusiastic, discovery-focused | "A collection matching your interests just dropped" |
| **Customer Support** | Empathetic, solution-focused | "I understand the urgency‚Äîlet's resolve this now" |
| **Error Messages** | Helpful, human | "We couldn't process that bid. Try again or contact support." |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Clear, precise | "Per our Seller Agreement, section 4.2..." |
| **Social Media** | Conversational, engaging | "That 1959 Les Paul just sold for $47K in 4 days" |

### By User Emotional State

| User State | Tone Response |
|------------|---------------|
| **Excited (won an item)** | Match their energy. Celebrate the acquisition. |
| **Anxious (first-time seller)** | Reassuring, concrete, specific steps. |
| **Frustrated (dispute)** | Empathy first. Then solution. "I understand this is frustrating..." |
| **Confused (how bidding works)** | Patient, clear, step-by-step. No judgment. |
| **Confident (experienced user)** | Efficient, direct. Don't slow them down. |

- "Keep 97%" (our fee structure mantra)

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Sentence Structure
- **Prefer short sentences.** Under 20 words when possible.
- **One idea per sentence.** Avoid compound complexity.
- **Active voice.** "We verify every seller" not "Every seller is verified by us."
- **Front-load important info.** Lead with the benefit or action.

### Contractions
**Use freely.** We're conversational, not formal.
- "We're" not "We are"
- "Don't" not "Do not"
- "Can't" not "Cannot"

Exception: Legal text, error messages, and formal notifications use full forms.

### Punctuation
- **Periods:** Use for complete thoughts. Even in UI microcopy.
- **Oxford comma:** Yes. "Verified, curated, and ready to bid."
- **Exclamation points:** Sparingly. Only for genuine excitement (win notifications, milestones).
- **Semicolons:** Avoid. Break into two sentences instead.

### Capitalization
- **Headlines:** Sentence case. "Sell your collection" not "Sell Your Collection"
- **Buttons:** Sentence case. "Place bid" not "Place Bid"
- **Product names:** No special styling. "Luxor Bids" not "LUXOR BIDS"
- **Acronyms:** All caps. "ID" "NFT" "UX"

### Numbers
- **Prices:** Always include dollar sign and commas. "$4,200" not "4200"
- **Percentages:** Use % symbol. "3%" not "three percent"
- **Time:** Use numbers. "7 days" not "seven days"
- **Big numbers:** Abbreviate with K/M/B. "$2.3M" not "$2,300,000"

### Emoji Policy
- **Marketing emails:** Occasional, relevant emoji (üèÜ for wins, ‚è∞ for deadlines)
- **Product UI:** Never
- **Support messages:** Rarely, only to match user energy
- **Social media:** Freely, but tastefully

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Product UI Copy

**Principles:**
1. **Scannable first.** Users are task-focused.
2. **Action-oriented.** Verbs before nouns.
3. **Context-aware.** Don't state the obvious.

**Examples:**
- ‚úÖ "Current bid: $4,200" (not "The current highest bid on this item is $4,200")
- ‚úÖ "Ends in 2 days" (not "This auction will close in 48 hours")
- ‚úÖ "Verify your ID to bid" (not "Please complete the identity verification process")

### Email Copy

**Subject Lines:**
- Under 50 characters when possible
- Front-load value: "Your collection sold" not "Update on your listing"
- Avoid spam triggers: "FREE!" "URGENT!" "ACT NOW!"

**Body:**
- Lead with the most important information
- One primary CTA per email
- Sign with a name (from the team)

### Support Copy

**Opening:** Acknowledge the issue specifically.  
**Middle:** Explain clearly, offer solutions.  
**Closing:** Confirm resolution, offer further help.

**Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

I see you're [issue]. That [validation].

Here's what we can do:
[Option 1]
[Option 2]

Let me know which works best for you.

[Name]
Luxor Bids Support
```

### Social Media

**Voice:** Most casual expression of our brand. Conversational, timely, engaging.

**Do:**
- Share wins: "That 1959 Les Paul just sold for $47K in 4 days üé∏"
- Ask questions: "What's the one piece that got away?"
- Celebrate collectors: "Welcome @vintagecameras to Luxor‚Äîcheck out their first collection"

**Don't:**
- Post just to post
- Use stock photos
- Engage with trolls

## Validation Checklist

Before publishing any content, run through this checklist:

- [ ] **Airport Test:** Would you recognize this as Luxor without seeing the logo?
- [ ] **Consistency Check:** Does this match our 5 voice attributes?
- [ ] **Audience Match:** Does this resonate with serious collectors?
- [ ] **Authenticity Test:** Does this feel true to who we are?
- [ ] **Differentiation Test:** Does this stand out from eBay/Auction houses?
- [ ] **Jargon Check:** Are we explaining terms or excluding people?
- [ ] **Length Check:** Can we say this in fewer words?
- [ ] **Active Voice:** Are we using passive constructions?
